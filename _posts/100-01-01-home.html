What and Who

At MathCamp, UGA faculty mentors each work with a graduate student helper, and and undergraduate helper guiding a group of roughly 5 high school students on exploratory projects in a range of topics. We plan to have 5 or 6 groups, for a total of 25-30 students.

Topics vary year to year, but in the past, they have included topology, graph theory, number theory, cryptography, mathematical problem solving, and mathematical biology.



When and Where

MathCamp 2016 will be running Monday, May 30 to Friday, June 3, in the Boyd Graduate Studies Research Building (200 D.W. Brooks Drive), at the University of Georgia Athens Campus.

Camp starts at 9:30am each day, and will run until 3:30pm, though participants will also have the option of staying until 5:30pm

.

How

MathCamp is funded by NSF CAREER grant DMS-1151252, and this grant pays for all student activities with the exception of food and t-shirts.

Participants will pay a fee of $40 at the beginning of camp to cover the expense of snacks and a t-shirt. Participants also have the option of either bringing their own bag lunch, or ordering from Jimmy John's or a similar local restaurant.
